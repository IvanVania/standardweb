Standard Size — Why is it important?

Standardized containers. Sea shipping existed long before container ships, but it was Malcolm McLean who revolutionized global trade by creating a standardized container size. This dramatically accelerated trade and boosted global GDP. Standardization made trade fast and reliable.

A similar example is railroads. Their rapid spread began with the adoption of a standard track gauge. Everyone knows how railroads transformed America and the world.

John D. Rockefeller started building pipelines to transport oil directly to refineries, making Standard Oil the largest company of its time and forever changing the U.S. economy.
